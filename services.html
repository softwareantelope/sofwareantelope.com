What Domains names are

Your domain is your unique identity on the web.

.com is the most common domain suffix, location specific suffixes such as .co.uk are also available, but now you can get a large range of meaningful suffixes. For example: .london, .gallery, .tech, .video and hundreds of others.

The www prefix usually points at your main website. You can also separate other zones of operation utilising sub-domains by using other prefixes: for example mail. or sales.

You may have registered a domain already, or we can register one for you. We will provide zone file configuration details to attach your services.

Hosting - resources available

Hosting a website need not be complicated. There are many options available but to offer you the best of both worlds, we support two basic forms of hosting, each has their own advantages. Click on the panels below for more information.

Shared Hosting

Shared Hosting provides many resources that are easily configured for you. Within a short period of time you can have a fully working CMS installation, customise a template that gives you immediate web presence. Email addresses and full site management are also available. We highly recommend WordPress for this purpose for its incredibly easy operation, rich set of template resources and plug in resources that support blogs, social media, ecommerce, galleries, publishing, and so much more.

Cloud Hosting

Once your basic website is operational and visible to the world with shared hosting, we can extend or move it to high availability, fast cloud based services. This is a better solution for busy sites and for specialised application development. If your site is high volume, we will quote the addition of required resources after monitoring site usage patterns.

Agile Development

A better way for stake-holders to track progress on their development projects. Software Antelope uses a range of online tools that are a) simple to use, and b) allow us to collaborate with our clients, directly. Agile development methodology is supported by a task board (we use Trello.com) and a software repository (see our github.com link under Contact). This enables us to a) plan out each stage of delivery with full client oversight of actions agreed, and b) to deliver secure updates that provide constant progress. Agile means constant reviewing of project goals, plus an ability to adapt to changing conditions and new ideas.

Waterfall Development

The "old fashioned" way to deliver software project is to plan every stage in a detailed specification document. This enables us to provide an estimate on the scope of a project. Tracking a waterfall project is less flexible as goals should not change. Usually, we would expect a waterfall project to a) take longer to achieve any goals, and b) be more expensive, overall. It is sometimes preferable to implement a solid idea, but in our changing world, Agile development is usually more appropriate.
